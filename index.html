Tawag ng Tanghalan

It is a singing competition for amateurs in the Philippines that currently airs as a segment of the noontime show It's Showtime on ABS-CBN. The competition, known as "Your All-Time Favorite Search for Outstanding Amateur Talents," is open to Filipino contestants from Metro Manila, Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao, as well as contestants from other countries. Additionally, it is still a competition for an event.

The regular season is divided into four quarters, with the semifinals taking place at the end of each quarter. The two semifinalists with the highest scores from each quarter, along with winners from the wildcard rounds, move on to compete in the grand finals.

Wanderland

(commonly referred to as Wanderland Music and Arts Festival), Wanderland was founded in 2013 by Karpos Multimedia Inc. Their vision is to make Filipino people experience a one of a kind music festival and make Wanderland, the biggest and the most established music festival in the Philippines. It also has a pre-event called Wanderband, it is Wanderland's annual battle of the local independent bands. The winner and runner-up are given the chance to play live at Wanderland Music and Arts Festival Since 2017, Wanderland launched a similar annual battle for live visual artists. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic in the Philippines, organizers announced on March 4, 2020, that the music festival would be indefinitely postponed in accordance with quarantine but they are making a comeback next year, on march 2023.

Bayan Bayanan: Letters from Home

A new musical about Filipinos working abroad is staged at the Tanghalang Nicanor Abelardo in CCP. Bayan Bayanan: Letters from Home is set in Europe, told through the perspective of a Filipino scholar sent to Geneva for further studies but is enamored with France and falls for a French girl. It will also tell stories of other Filipinos and their experience building a "community of home" wherever they are. The portrayal of different kinds of Filipinos abroad is initially refreshing, giving the audience a glimpse into a more diverse range of experiences as opposed to the usual portrayal of the diaspora as a mass of miserable, mistreated, underpaid, and overworked domestic workers or caregivers, all struggling to make ends meet while dealing with discrimination and fear of capture. It’s a treat to show that Filipinos are not always poor, that they can afford certain luxuries of life and their hard work can sometimes pay off. But it’s in this level of privilege that the play’s greatest flaws are exposed.

Joseph the Dreamer

Based on the biblical story of Jacob's son Joseph, "Joseph the Dreamer" was one of the musicals that finished its full run in Manila before the onset of the pandemic in March 2020. It was also set to be staged in Cebu in May of that year but was no longer pushed through due to lockdown restrictions. The musical by Trumpets is now back for a restaging, again featuring Sam Concepcion in the lead role. Gary Valenciano, who once played the titular role in 1999, returns to the stage this year now as Jacob. Joseph the Dreamer is an imaginative immersion into the journey of a fascinating character, replete with both substance and spectacle, from the strong emotional beats to the moving music and creative choreography. The timeless tale is given new life by a production that deftly balances modernization with the presentation of familiar themes and characters. As a well-known Biblical story, Joseph could have suffered from the audience’s familiarity with the source material so it was paramount for the company to find a fresh take on the story and this production has largely succeeded in this endeavor. The show has all the hallmarks of memorable musical theater from catchy musical numbers, inventive choreography and production design, distinctive characters, and a gripping story.

ManilART

ManilART is the first and longest-running art fair in the Philippines. Established in 2009, its mandate is to promote Philippine Culture through the visual arts. A project co-presented by the National Commission on Culture and the Arts (NCCA), ManilART's vision is to make the Philippines a premier art hub in Asia. They have brought traditions into the new age with their theme of Continuing Legacies. This year, ManilART2022 continues forward Forging Futures. Forging Futures is about embracing the new wave of art while bringing our artistic heritage to the forefront of technology. The National Art Fair ran from October 19-23 at the SMX Aura Convention Center and was available for 360 virtual viewing at www.manilartfair.com.

Editorial

The fact that there will never be a perfect price for performing and visual arts is one of the big challenges for their promotion. One of the most contentious issues in all of arts and entertainment is pricing, and it always will be. Price too low and you leave money on the table; price too much and you are considered "arrogant." If you don't make enough money, your funded programs that contribute to sustaining demand will certainly suffer. This has made it difficult to fill up venues. Marketing is a serious problem since performing arts marketing is often situational; what can you do if you don't receive positive reviews? Maintaining a challenging, rewarding, and profitable program to draw new customers and keep current ones engaged is more difficult. It's more difficult than ever to run a successful arts organization, especially if you live in a market where the arts haven't traditionally been as popular as in other places.

Keeping up with talent is the two-edged challenge every artist experiences. However, talent or skills never vanishes and never fades away, but it needs continuous practice. There can be different levels of your talent, and if you reached an advanced level and stopped practicing you might find yourself back to being a beginner all over again. Also, that would be a nightmare to any person, because every single improvement counts, big time. So, keep on practicing, you amazing artists and believe that your talent has no limits.Usually of course, ambitiousness is a quality we should strive for but it can also lead us to set unrealistic goals for ourselves- a frequent cause of work-related stress. Finding balance between focusing on what your organization actually does as well as promoting it is difficult to do but it isn’t impossible. A strong brand identity should be something you invest in. This is a word often thrown about, its definition being ‘of undisputed origin and not a copy; genuine.’ It’s highly likely that you work in the arts because you are passionate about them. Transfer this passion into your marketing. Be authentic when you choose how best to get your message out. Don’t be afraid to be creative and try something new! Think about how you personally engage with online marketing- has a particular organization posted content you have enjoyed? If so, think about why you tapped that ‘like’ button. Did it make you laugh? Did it start a conversation? Or was it just a great photo?

The arts have always stood for beauty, a connection to others, prosperity, creativity, and the ability to express oneself. Arts may provide beauty to a space, strengthen community identities, and challenge the status quo through arousing the senses, emotions, and thoughts. With all these alternatives, it is also possible to integrate the arts' creativity and teamwork into the free market economy to generate employment and profit. About Us

Manifesto (Short form)

We at Arete believe nothing in art is new or old fashioned, only good or bad. We shall preserve the visual and performing arts of our country. It pushes us to constantly reimagine how we can encourage the young and old to create art. For you. For all the artists you want to see and for the people you want to exhibit your art to. For all the things you want to get. For all the ways you want to earn in the Philippines or around the world. We are here to inherent excellence in all things as it should be.

Manifesto (Long form)

Arete is a big believer in making art, making noise, and making a ripple in the world. Through that expression, we communicate by drawing on our own unique emotions, thoughts, and experiences. When you see and study another's art, you're seeing the world through their eyes. When you create, you're letting the world see through yours. Even if your work/performance is never displayed in art galleries, stages, exhibits, or museums, personal expression holds many benefits. Creating an artistic piece is relaxing and fulfilling for many artists and helps them cope with the pressures of everyday life or extreme situations. In many ways, making stuff is the opposite of mental distress. Often, when we're feeling bad, we turn away from our inner selves. We close up shop. But making stuff it's about listening to yourself, hearing a part of yourself, and transforming it into something external. Something for others to enjoy. Something that now exists apart from yourself. Something that proves: hey, I am here. I am alive. I matter. I made a thing.

That’s why we at Arete are tasked with improving the lives of the younger generation. What better way to do this than by helping them to develop a love of the arts? Art in all its forms whether through theater, music, sculpture, or painting is something that should be cherished and appreciated by everyone young and old, and developing this passion for the arts begins in you. It’s a key part for us to promote a deeper understanding of the arts so that the younger generation becomes more self-aware and self-confident, begins to broaden their horizons, and develops a wider range of skills and abilities.

We think this stuff is important. We know it will inspire people, especially the younger generation. We know it will help other people who might be at the very tentative stages of channeling their emotions into art or music, to create something of their own.

What is our main goal in promoting the Creative Economy to JSH and SHS students?

We are here to promote; Knowing the importance and influence of performance and visual arts can be life changing. It may teach you many skills in addition to being aesthetic to the eye, enabling people to be unique and confident. To encourage the young folks' creativity skills and give them opportunities to find like-minded individuals to share their talents with. Becoming beneficial for our community as well.

What separates us from other companies that promote Creative Economy? What is our added value?

Arete is a convenient and affordable place to buy tickets at a discounted price for concerts, festivals, art fairs, and theater. We protect buyers from overpricing. As a buyer, you know you’ll always be buying a fair price for your tickets.

What are the underserved segments of your target market? What are their pain points and how can your company address these?

The Philippines has a large and active community for artists. Arete has recognized that it is hard to promote ourselves as artists and deciding to become a professional is something that can be quite overwhelming and confusing at the start of your journey. You might not know where to start! Artists are all different from one another, and as with anything in this life - there is no one way of doing things. With everything becoming expensive we are here to offer discounts on any kind of event. Be it theater, concerts or art fairs. We are here to give recognition to local artists. Making it more accessible and convenient; enabling the community in the performing and visual arts to communicate with one another.
